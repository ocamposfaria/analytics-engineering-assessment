Data and dictionary

Dataset

Sourced from the dbt seed raw_leads: one row per lead.

Business context

Goal is to convert leads to a subscription (signed_up).

Purpose of this analysis

Surface funnel friction and opportunities by agent and lead source.

Coverage and limitations

Test leads are excluded from KPIs; qualified_flag is not the same as a closed sale.

Leads by stage (snapshot)

Each lead has one status at a time: the bars are mutually exclusive buckets, not a single queue where every lead visits every stage. For example, No Response ends that lead’s cycle—it does not imply a demo or proposal follows.

In Volume by current state, bars are ordered like the journey above (from Contacted through the pipeline, with Signed Up and Rejected last). Counts are still a snapshot, not a measured flow between states.

Conceptual journey (illustrative): from Contacted a lead may go to No Response or Demo Completed; after the demo it may become Unqualified or continue through proposal, negotiation, and Pending Signature to Signed Up or Rejected.

The dots suggest possible paths. In real data, Rejected can occur at other points too; the numeric charts remain a snapshot (one state per lead).
